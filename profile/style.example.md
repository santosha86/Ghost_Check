# Style & Constraints — Rohan Mehta

## Work authorisation and location

Indian national, currently on a KSA Iqama sponsored by my current employer (Gulf AI Advisory). Relocation within the Gulf (UAE, Qatar, Bahrain) is straightforward. Relocation to the US, UK, or EU is possible but requires visa sponsorship clearly stated in the JD — roles that don't explicitly mention sponsorship are almost certainly not viable for me, even if the JD says "open to international candidates."

Fully remote is fine. Hybrid — one or two days a week on site in the Gulf — is fine. On-site only in a location I'd need to relocate to requires the visa + comp combination to work, not just one of them.

## Seniority floor

I'm not taking a Senior IC role, even at higher comp. The floor is Principal IC (individual contributor owning technical architecture across three or more teams) or Director (managing two or more AI/ML teams with P&L responsibility). This is not about title prestige — it's about role scope. A Senior IC role would mean I stop owning architecture decisions, and that is the part of the work I find meaningful and that compounds into my Doctorate research.

Roles labelled "Lead" or "Staff" I evaluate case by case — the title varies between companies. What matters: do I own the architecture, and am I accountable for outcomes at the programme level?

## Sector preferences and anti-preferences

Most of my career has been IT-services delivery — embedded in client enterprises through Gulf consultancies. I'm aware that this reads as a silent downgrade at product companies and in the West (the "IT-services discount"). I want GhostCheck's `it-services-discount` agent to flag this explicitly whenever it applies, rather than assume I already know.

**Target:** product companies (AI platforms, developer tools, enterprise SaaS), enterprise in-house AI roles (as an AI architect at a bank, airline, or energy company — not as a vendor), and deep-tech startups with real engineering problems. I'm not filtering services out — a Principal architect role at a top-tier consultancy is attractive if the work is genuinely architecture and not delivery staff-aug.

**Explicit anti-preferences:** pure staff-augmentation roles, roles where "AI" is a buzzword layered over a reporting dashboard, and companies whose primary product is mass surveillance or adversarial data brokerage.

## Compensation and notice

**Salary floor:** roughly SAR 50,000 per month base in the Gulf (about USD 160,000/year), or USD 220,000/year base equivalent in the US. Bonus and equity on top. I'm flexible on the base-vs-bonus-vs-equity split for the right company, but not on the total-comp floor.

**Notice period:** 60–90 days from my current employer. Roles that require a sub-30-day start are a soft no — I'd rather start correctly than rush.

**Relocation package:** expected for any role requiring a physical move. I'm not paying for my own international move.

## Narrative preferences

Building in public is a priority. I publish about architecture decisions, write long-form pieces on agentic systems, and speak at Gulf AI events. Roles with blanket NDAs on all work — no blog posts, no talks, no open-source contribution — are a soft blocker; I'd consider them only at significantly higher comp and for a short defined stint.

Pursuing a Doctorate in Emerging Technologies (Generative AI specialisation) part-time. This means time-boxed commitments like "80-hour weeks for six months" are a real problem, not a nice-to-have to avoid. I can commit to normal senior-role intensity (50–55 hours per week) indefinitely, and to short crunches of a few weeks when the work demands it. I cannot commit to sustained overwork without the Doctorate failing.

**Working style:** I work best with fully-distributed, async-first teams. I've led AI squads across KSA, India, and remote contributors; synchronous "everyone in the same room at 9 a.m." cultures slow me down. This is a preference, not a blocker — but it should be weighed into `channel-mix` and `posting-decoder` verdicts where culture signals are visible in the JD.
